What the IRS Doesn't Want You to Know
🏢
Entity Structure is Everything
Most LLCs are structured wrong. An LLC taxed as an S-Corp at $100K+ profit can save $5,000–$15,000/year in self-employment tax alone — just by splitting income between salary and distributions. The IRS won't tell you this.
🏠
The Home Office Strategy
If you use a dedicated space exclusively for business, you can deduct a percentage of your rent/mortgage, utilities, internet, and insurance. S-Corp owners can be reimbursed tax-free through an accountable plan — money out of the business, tax-free in your pocket.
📈
Retire Rich AND Cut Your Tax Bill
A Solo 401(k) lets self-employed owners contribute up to $70,000/year tax-free (2025). At a 30% tax rate, maxing it saves $21,000 in taxes. Pair with ShareBuilder 401k for easy setup.
🚗
Vehicle & Mileage Deductions
The 2025 standard mileage rate is 70¢/mile. If you drive 15,000 business miles, that's a $10,500 deduction. Alternatively, buy an SUV over 6,000 lbs GVWR and use Section 179 to deduct 100% of the purchase price in year one (up to $2.5M under OBBBA 2025).
