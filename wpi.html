The Call Flow.

WPI hands you a warm intro. Close the merchant deal first. Then ask 3 questions that fork the upsell.

Open with a Lead-In (30 sec) Pick from the Lead-Ins tab. Don't read it — paraphrase. Establish that WPI Rewards turns processing into perks rather than just a cost.
Discover & Qualify (3-5 min) Get current rate, monthly volume, average ticket. Then ask the three magic questions below to surface upsell angles. Capture answers in Call Notes tab.
Position WPI Rewards + CDP (3 min) Their qualifying rate (3.99% / no SSOC) unlocks the rewards platform free. CDP passes fees to card-paying customers. Address rebuttals from the Rebuttals tab.
Close the merchant deal (paperwork) Coversheet + merchant app. This is your bonus deal — protect it. Don't sabotage by overloading the upsell yet.
Pivot to "while we're at it…" (2 min) Now use what you learned in discovery. Match their employee size + industry to the upsell ladder in the next tabs. Pick ONE upsell to suggest, not all of them.
Book the follow-up (1 min) Either schedule a separate call for the upsell, or send them the relevant link with a note: "Take a look — we'll review on Friday." Send the VIP trip link for goodwill.
★ The 3 Magic Discovery Questions

Ask these EVERY time during qualifying:

  1. How many employees do you have? — forks the entire upsell ladder. Capture the actual number, not "a few."
  2. Do you own the building or rent? — unlocks Maven Cost Seg + commercial REACH Solar.
  3. If we eliminated $50K of hidden expense for you this year, what would you do with it? — qualifies them for The Ownly Breakthrough; their answer reveals their personal driver.
WPI training rule: in-person beats phone. If the appointment is in their territory and the relationship justifies it, drive to them. Field activity is the only "real" activity per the Field Sales Success Guide.

Rebuttals Cheat Sheet.

The seven objections you'll hear. Answer with confidence — they're already pre-tested.

"Why do I need to be at a specific rate to qualify for the rewards program?"
Our rewards program is designed to be a high-value benefit for our merchants, where every dollar processed helps you earn rewards. The rate criteria ensure the program is sustainable, allowing us to offer premium rewards like travel, luxury goods, and more. This structure helps us keep the program free for qualifying merchants.
"I'm currently on a lower rate and don't want to increase it just for the program."
I understand wanting to keep costs low, and our rewards program actually provides significant value that can offset a small rate adjustment. Many of our merchants find the rewards they earn cover much more than any rate difference, especially with options like flights, hotels, and electronics. Plus, you'll still have the same level of processing efficiency with additional perks.
"Why can't traditional rate merchants participate?"
The program is designed to benefit merchants with specific rate structures. Traditional plans, with their unique pricing, don't align as closely with our rewards model. However, I'd be happy to look at how a small adjustment could open up access to these valuable rewards without a significant increase in your processing costs.
"I don't see the value in paying $45 a month just to join the program."
That's a great question. The $45 monthly fee actually covers access to an extensive rewards platform where you can redeem benefits on almost everything processed through your business. Think of it as a monthly investment in perks that reward your business growth directly. Merchants often earn rewards worth far more than the membership fee, so it quickly pays for itself.
"I'm happy with my current processor and not sure this is worth the switch."
We value long-term relationships, and our WPI Rewards program is one way we add consistent value for our merchants beyond simple processing. We're offering a rewards experience to increase engagement and reward your business, not just process payments. If you're considering the potential perks and our service quality, it might be worth seeing how this could positively impact your bottom line.
"Is there any way to join the program without meeting the rate requirements?"
Right now, the rate requirements are in place to keep the rewards program free for qualifying merchants. Meeting these criteria means you get access to all the rewards benefits without additional fees. If it's helpful, we can explore how adjusting your rate might impact your business costs and find a solution that works.
"Will joining the program lock me into a long-term agreement?"
Not at all. The WPI Rewards program is designed to be a flexible benefit. You'll have full access to rewards as long as you're at the qualifying rate, but there's no long-term lock-in specific to the rewards. It's all about adding value to your business.
Bridge phrase — when you've handled the objection and want to move forward: "Makes sense? OK, let me grab the coversheet and we'll get this set up." Don't ask permission. Move.

Upsell Ladder by Employee Size.

The single biggest fork. Ask "how many employees?" early — it tells you which Ownly upsells fit and which would waste their time.

1–9 employees · Micro

Owner-Operator

The owner IS the business. Personal financial wins are business wins. Most upsells should center on the owner's wallet.
  • Local Spotlight 9x12 — $497/$997, fills slot exclusivity in their city
  • Tax Hive — $10K tax-savings guarantee for the owner
  • WealthPath subscription — for the owner personally
  • REACH Solar — owner's home (residential, no commercial complexity)
  • Freedom Score — free 7-min diagnostic, owner takes it
  • The Ownly Breakthrough — DREAMS Score for the business
10–49 employees · Small

Has Payroll, Maybe Benefits

Now we're talking real numbers. Payroll opens Section 125. Building ownership opens cost seg. Multiple employees means perks programs deliver retention ROI.
  • Section 125 Plan — pre-tax payroll savings (DREAMS B2B)
  • Bank Breezy — B2B banking optimization
  • Maven Cost Seg — IF they own the building
  • Perks platform — $197/mo employee retention play
  • Retirement planning — $297/mo, owner + employees
  • R&D credits — IF they manufacture, build, or develop
  • + everything from Micro
50–249 employees · Mid

Has HR Function

There's a person whose job is to find programs like ours. Speaker bookings become realistic. DREAMS engagements become deeper. Foundation play opens up.
  • Speaker booking — pitch the 30-45 min Breakout to HR for lunch-and-learns first
  • Deeper DREAMS engagement — full business X-ray, not just the free diagnostic
  • Foundation/grant play — Bebop research if they have a corporate foundation
  • Wellness program partnership — health/telehealth at $147/mo
  • Multi-location bots — automation if they have multiple sites
  • + everything from Small
250+ employees · Large

Full Enterprise

CWA-tier territory. Fiduciary engagements. Full speaker keynote. This is where the Campbell WA precedent applies.
  • CWA-style fiduciary advisory — reference the signed Campbell WA proposal
  • Full keynote booking — "Own Nothing, Control Everything" 45-60 min at corporate event
  • Executive coaching engagement — for C-suite
  • Foundation lead-gen via Bebop — if they're a foundation, board, or trust
  • Multi-site Local Spotlight — across all locations
  • + everything from Mid
Don't pitch the whole ladder. Pick the ONE upsell that matches what they revealed in discovery. Save the rest for follow-ups. A second meeting is more valuable than a longer first one.
